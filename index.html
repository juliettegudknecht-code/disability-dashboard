Celebrating 50+ Years of IDEA

In 1975 the Education for All Handicapped Children Act, the law we now know as IDEA, promised every child with a disability a free, appropriate public education. With it came a national commitment to count every child, every year. More than five decades on, this dashboard marks 50+ years of IDEA Section 618 data collection, from the first child count in 1976–77 to the figures reported for 2024–25.

OSEP, within the Office of Special Education and Rehabilitative Services (OSERS), is dedicated to improving results for infants, toddlers, children, and youth with disabilities, ages birth through 21, by providing leadership and financial support to assist States/entities, local districts, and programs. Section 618 of IDEA requires that each State/entity submit data about the infants and toddlers, birth through age 2, who receive early intervention services under Part C of IDEA, and students with disabilities, ages 3 through 21, who receive special education and related services under Part B of IDEA.

About these data. Section 618 of the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) (20 U.S.C. § 1418; 34 C.F.R. §§ 300.640–300.646, 303.720–303.724) requires each State to report annually to the U.S. Department of Education on children and youth with disabilities. This dashboard presents the Child Count and Educational Environments collection in depth (SY 1976–77 through SY 2024–25), together with summary measures from the Discipline, Exiting, Personnel, and Assessment collections (Part B) and the Part C early-intervention collections for the most recent reporting year available. The remaining collections are linked, with the reporting years available, in “Explore the data, by collection” below. Suppressed values are identified in accordance with the legend in the footer.

Geographic scope. Except where a chart states otherwise, all figures cover the 50 States and the District of Columbia (50 + DC). They exclude Puerto Rico, the other U.S. outlying areas, the Bureau of Indian Education, and the freely associated states, which are reported separately and are listed in Additional entities reporting under IDEA Section 618 below.

About "States": reporting scope & entities
Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, the term State includes the 50 states, the District of Columbia, the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico, and the outlying areas. Throughout this dashboard, "State Data Displays" cover the 50 states and the District of Columbia, abbreviated 50 + DC, which is the reporting universe with complete Section 618 data. The outlying areas (Guam, the U.S. Virgin Islands, American Samoa, and the Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands), the Bureau of Indian Education, the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico, and the freely associated states (the Federated States of Micronesia, the Republic of the Marshall Islands, and the Republic of Palau) report a Part B child count but are not included in the other Section 618 collections; the U.S. Department of Education reports them separately within the aggregate "U.S., Outlying Areas, and Freely Associated States." Use the Include Outlying Areas and Freely Associated States toggle in the top navigation to add their Part B child count, ages 3–21, SY 2024–25.

Explore the Data by Collection · All 12 Collections

There are 12 data collections authorized under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) Section 618, 8 under Part B and 4 under Part C. Child Count and Educational Environments (Part B), and Child Count and Settings (Part C), were reported as separate collections through 2011–12 and have been collected together since 2012–13, so a few separate historical files also appear below. This dashboard charts the Child Count and Educational Environments data in depth, plus headline views from several other collections; every collection is linked here for you to explore at data.ed.gov.

Additional Entities Reporting Under IDEA Section 618

Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, Section 618, data are reported to the U.S. Department of Education's Office of Special Education Programs (OSEP) by 60 Part B reporting entities: the 50 States, the District of Columbia, the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico, the Bureau of Indian Education, the U.S. outlying areas, and the freely associated states. The charts throughout this dashboard cover the 50 States and the District of Columbia. The additional entities listed below are reported by the U.S. Department of Education within the aggregate “U.S., Outlying Areas, and Freely Associated States” and are therefore not included in the figures presented elsewhere. Counts shown are children and youth ages 3 through 21 served under IDEA, Part B, for school year 2024–25. Reporting responsibility also differs by part: Part B data are reported by each State Education Agency, while under Part C, early-intervention data are reported by each state's designated Lead Agency, the agency the Governor appoints to administer Part C, which is not always the State Education Agency.
